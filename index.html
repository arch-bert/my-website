About Me

I'm in my final year of a Bcom Mathematical Science degree at Stellenbosch University, majoring in Computer Science and Financial Risk Management. My journey started with three years in Actuarial Science, where I discovered my passion for programming, statistics, data, risk, and algorithms.

My goal is to work on highly optimized trading algorithms or develop financial software, combining my love for coding with my interest in finance. I've always been fascinated by data, often rating movies on IMDb and books on Goodreads. Recently, I've become interested in AI and machine learning and plan to dive deeper into these fields using Python.

Throughout my degree, I've become proficient in Java and C, and I've independently mastered Python, which I prefer for its simplicity and powerful data libraries. Some of my notable Python projects include a Card Counting Simulation, Financial Data Extractor with OpenBB, and Asset Correlation Analysis.

I enjoy learning and spend much of my free time reading, listening to audiobooks, and engaging with podcasts. I believe in the continuous pursuit of knowledge and perspective, always striving to expand my understanding and skills.
